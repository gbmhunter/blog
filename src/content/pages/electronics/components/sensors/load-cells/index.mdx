Load cells are sensors that measure force or weight. They contain sensitive strain gauges attached to a substrate such as a metal beam. An applied force causes small deformations in the substrate which in turn changes the resistance of the strain gauge. This change in [resistance](/electronics/components/resistors/) is measured with a [Wheatstone bridge circuit](/electronics/circuit-design/wheatstone-bridges/) and converted to a digital signal using an ADC. Knowing the sensitivity of the strain gauge (typically $0.5-2\,mV/V$ for the cell's rated capacity), this voltage is converted to a force or weight. They can easily measure both tension and compression.

- Canister
