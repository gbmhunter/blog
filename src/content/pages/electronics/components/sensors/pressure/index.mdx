Pressure sensors measure the pressure against a surface. They can be used to measure the pressure of a gas, fluid or solid.

## Pressure Sensing Film

Pressure sensing film is a material which permanently changes colour based on applied pressure. The only manufacturer I am aware of that makes this is Fujifilm with their "Prescale" brand. The film contains microcapsules of colour-forming material. When broken under pressure, they react with colour-developing material, causing the film to change from white to a magenta colour (as shown in the image below). The colour intensity (which is referred to as the colour density) is proportional to the pressure applied.

<Image src={import('./_assets/fujifilm-prescale-how-it-works.webp')} width="700px">How Prescale pressure sensing film works.[^fujifilm-visual-material-solutions]</Image>

There are the following products in the Prescale range:[^fujifilm-prescale-film]

| Product (code) | Pressure range | Type | Roll-type Product Size | Sheet-type Product Size |
|----------------|----------------|------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| Ultra extreme low pressure (5LW) | 0.006-0.05 MPa | Dual-sheet | 320 mm x 2 m | - |
| Extreme low pressure (4LW) | 0.05-0.2 MPa | Dual-sheet | 320 mm x 3 m | - |
| Ultra super low pressure (LLLW) | 0.2 - 0.6 MPa | Dual-sheet | 270 mm x 5 m | 270 x 200 mm (5 sheets) |
| Super low pressure (LLW) | 0.5 - 2.5 MPa | Dual-sheet | 270 mm x 6 m | 270 x 200 mm (5 sheets) |
| Low pressure (LW) | 2.5 - 10 MPa | Dual-sheet | 270 mm x 10 m | 270 x 200 mm (5 sheets) |
| Medium pressure (MW) | 10 - 50 MPa | Dual-sheet | 270 mm x 10 m | - |
| Medium pressure (MS) | 10 - 50 MPa | Mono-sheet | 270 mm x 10 m | 270 x 200 mm (5 sheets) |
| Higher pressure (HS) | 50 - 130 MPa | Mono-sheet | 270 mm x 10 m | 270 x 200 mm (5 sheets) |
| Super high pressure (HHS) | 130 - 300 MPa | Mono-sheet | 270 mm x 10 m | 270 x 200 mm (5 sheets) |

All of the dual-sheet product codes end in `W` and all of the mono-sheet product codes end in `S`. The dual-sheet products come with an A film and a C film. The A film contains the colour-forming material (microcapsules) and the C film is coated with a colour-developing material. The coated sides of these films must be facing each other. When getting an intuitive sense for pressures, it's useful to remember that $1\,\text{MPa} = 1\,\text{N/mm}^2 = 10\,\text{kgf/cm}^2$.

To convert from colour density to pressure, charts are given which provide a mapping at various temperature and humidity conditions. The film is quite sensitive to both temperature and humidity, so it is important to take these into account when using the charts.

<Image src={import('./_assets/prescale-llw-instruction-manual-charts.webp')} width="900px">Charts for converting from colour density to pressure for the LLW Prescale film.[^fujifilm-prescale-llw-instruction-manual]</Image>

There is also special film in the Prescale range for operating at high temperatures (180 - 220°C). It appears that it is only available in the LLW pressure range (0.5 - 2.5 MPa).[^fujifilm-visual-material-solutions]

## Examples

### Honeywell ABP2 Series

The Honeywell ABP2 series is a family of board-mount pressure sensors. There are both through-hole and SMD versions. They detect pressure using piezoresistive silicon and offer both analogue and digital output options (digital versions support either the [I2C](/electronics/communication-protocols/i2c-communication-protocol) or [SPI](/electronics/communication-protocols/spi-communication-protocol) bus protocols). Options exist to both measure gas and liquid (there is an additional gel coating added to the liquid versions).[^honeywell-abp2-product-page]

<Image src={import('./_assets/photo-of-abp2-variants.webp')} width="500px">A photo of the various variants of the Honeywell ABP2 series pressure sensors.[^honeywell-abp2-datasheet]</Image>

You have to be careful when both soldering and handling these sensors. They are particularly sensitive to over-heating (the plastic parts and/or sealant can melt) during soldering. The plastic barb can also be torn of the rest of the component easily it too much torque/force is applied from the connected hose.

<Image src={import('./_assets/photo-of-abp2-air-pressure-sensors-on-pcb.webp')} width="600px">A photo of 3 Honeywell ABP2LAN air pressure sensors mounted on a PCB. Two are connected to hoses (with some sealant applied), one is not.</Image>
