## Three-Pin Shunt Regulators

A three-pin shunt voltage regulator is typically drawn as a [Zener diode](/electronics/components/diodes/zener-diodes/) with a third pin coming from the middle and out to the side. 

<Image src={import('./_assets/three-pin-shunt-regulator-schematic-symbol.png')} width="300px">The schematic symbol for a three-pin shunt voltage regulator, with the pin names also shown.</Image>

This can be confusing, as a three-pin shunt regulator is an IC (integrated circuit) that typically contains an [op-amp](/electronics/components/op-amps/) and a [BJT](/electronics/components/transistors/bipolar-junction-transistors-bjts/) along with a precision [voltage reference](/electronics/components/power-regulators/voltage-references/)! This is also reflected by the `U` IC designator prefix which is commonly used for these devices. The following image shows what actually is inside one of these devices:

<Image src={import('./_assets/three-pin-shunt-regulator-functional-diagram-ti.png')} width="300px">The internal functional diagram of a three-pin shunt regulator. Note that there is much more to one of these than just a simple Zener diode! Image from the LM431 datasheet.[^ti-lm431-datasheet]</Image>


## Manufacturer Part Numbers

- **431**: Very common shunt regulator.
- **LM431**: Shunt regulator from Texas Instruments based on the 431 series.
